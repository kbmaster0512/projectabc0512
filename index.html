Scene-agnostic Hierarchical Bimanual Task Planning via Visual Affordance Reasoning

Kwang Bin Lee, Jiho Kang, Sung-Hee Lee
KAIST Graduate School of Culture and Technology
arXiv preprint
arXiv

We propose a unified scene-agnostic bimanual task-planning framework that augments scenes with object points (blue) and interaction points (orange), allowing an embodied agent to interpret a user-defined task command, navigate to object points, and perform bimanual actions at interaction points.

Abstract

Embodied agents operating in open environments must translate high-level instructions into grounded, executable behaviors, often requiring coordinated use of both hands. While recent foundation models offer strong semantic reasoning, existing robotic task planners remain predominantly unimanual and fail to address the spatial, geometric, and coordination challenges inherent to bimanual manipulation in scene-agnostic settings. We present a unified framework for scene-agnostic bimanual task planning that bridges high-level reasoning with 3D-grounded two-handed execution. Our approach integrates three key modules. Visual Point Grounding (VPG) analyzes a single scene image to detect relevant objects and generate world-aligned interaction points. Bimanual Subgoal Planner (BSP) reasons over spatial adjacency and cross-object accessibility to produce compact, motion-neutralized subgoals that exploit opportunities for coordinated two-handed actions. Interaction-Point–Driven Bimanual Prompting (IPBP) binds these subgoals to a structured skill library, instantiating synchronized unimanual or bimanual action sequences that satisfy hand-state and affordance constraints. Together, these modules enable agents to plan semantically meaningful, physically feasible, and parallelizable two-handed behaviors in cluttered, previously unseen scenes. Experiments show that it produces coherent, feasible, and compact two-handed plans, and generalizes to cluttered scenes without retraining, demonstrating robust scene-agnostic affordance reasoning for bimanual tasks.

Method

Our proposed VPG processes a 3D scene by first constructing a grounded understanding of the environment during a preprocessing stage. Given the scene, a coarse scene label (e.g., "Cafe" or "Convenience Store"), and a list of candidate task commands, the module identifies the objects and interaction locations that are likely to matter for the upcoming task, outputting a set of object points and interaction points that are then augmented into the scene.

Given the grounded scene interpretation from VPG, the system generates a full bimanual plan for the user-defined task. The Bimanual Subgoal Planner (BSP) first determines where actions should occur by selecting an ordered set of object points and assigning subgoals and skills consistent with scene geometry and two-handed reachability. The Interaction-Point-Driven Bimanual Prompting module (IPBP) then produces synchronized bimanual action sequences at each point based on feasible contact options. Together, these stages form a spatially grounded, coordination-aware bimanual task plan.

Evaluation

We evaluated our framework across six task and scene pairs, demonstrating its ability to generalize bimanual manipulation planning to diverse real-world scenarios.

Our framework achieves consistently high success across all scene–task pairs (85–100%) with low operation counts (2.00–3.06), demonstrating efficient and generalizable bimanual planning.

Ablations show that all proposed modules are essential: removing BSP drops success from 93.33% to 71.66%, removing Skill RAG (a component of IPBP) collapses it to 18.33%, and removing VPG results in 0%. The full system substantially outperforms all variants.
